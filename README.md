Hogwarts Confectionery — Game Guide
Hogwarts Confectionery is a magical twist on the classic Mastermind puzzle game, dressed up in the robes of the wizarding world. Your goal is to decode a secret cake recipe hidden by Dumbledore himself — guided only by a cryptic riddle and the colour-coded clues your guesses reveal. Think less Purble Place, more Potions class meets Honeydukes.
The Objective
Decode the 4-ingredient secret cake recipe before you run out of attempts. Each recipe has one ingredient per slot — Base Layer, Flavour, Frosting, and Topping.
How to Play:
Step 1 — Read the Riddle
At the start of every round, Dumbledore leaves a poetic riddle in the left pane. It describes all four secret ingredients in disguised, story-like language. Read it carefully — it holds the answer if you're clever enough to see it.
Step 2 — Build your guess
Click any ingredient from the shelves on the right. Each shelf corresponds to one slot — Base, Flavour, Frosting, or Topping. Click a filled slot to clear it and swap it out.
Step 3 — Cast the Spell
Once all four slots are filled, hit ✨ Cast the Spell ✨ to submit your guess.
Step 4 — Read the clues
After each guess, two things update:

The Potion Log shows your guess as emojis alongside coloured dots
The Enchanted Clue Scroll gives you written, plain-English clues about what was right or wrong
The dots mean:
DotMeaning🟢 GreenCorrect ingredient in the correct slot🟡 GoldRight ingredient, but in the wrong slot⚫ DarkThat ingredient is not in the recipe at allStep 5 — Repeat
You have 6 attempts (shown as blood drop hearts 🩸). Use the riddle plus the dot clues to narrow it down. Solve it in fewer tries to earn more House Points!
- Scoring
Solving the recipe earns House Points
The fewer attempts you use, the more points you score
Your Best score is tracked across rounds
Each new round brings a fresh riddle and a new secret recipe
- Tips for New Players
Start with the riddle — it almost always tells you all four answers if you read it metaphorically. A "golden airy cloud" likely means Sponge. A "serpent's coiling root" is Licorice.
Green dots lock in — if you get a green dot on a slot, keep that ingredient there in your next guess.
Gold dots mean shuffle — try the same ingredient in a different slot.
All dark dots? Eliminate those four ingredients entirely from future guesses.
The Enchanted Clue Scroll names the ingredients plainly after each guess, so it gets easier as you go.
